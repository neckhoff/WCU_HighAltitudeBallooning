CSK CanSat

In early February 2022, Dr. Gomez and Nathan Eckhoff met with Twiggs Space Lab to discuss their CanSat and Cubito educational products, and six weeks later we received both a CanSat and Cubito kit.

The CanSat, short for Can Satellite, is a build-it-yourself educational tool to teach students the basics of satellite development. A printed circuit board for each critical subsystem is provided as well as all the parts. Students must solder each component in to its appropriate placement according to the provided user manual. Once fully assembled, the size of the CanSat is smaller than the size of a soda can.

Starting in mid-May, Nathan Eckhoff, began assembling the CanSat. Each subsystem came with three 6-pin stackable headers so the BUS signals could communicate between them. The Bottom Plate Adapter contained hardware to give the rest of the CanSat a way to attach onto an external piece of hardware such as a payload deployment system.

The Command and Data Handling Subsystem contains a MicroSD chip for datalogging, a Xiao Seeeduino microcontroller, an EEPROM, and a few other circuit components. The microcontroller communicates with the two integrated BUS systems, an I2C and a MISO/MOSI.

The Electrical Power Subsystem contains a premade, custom Battery Module which helps distribute the power from the battery to the correct terminals, charges the battery via microUSB, routes power out so a different system can harness power from the CanSat, and can also accept a small Solar Panel for power regeneration. This is also where the hardware lies to power on the CanSat via crossing two rails with a Long Jumper connector.

The Attitude Determination Subsystem houses a breakout board with an accelerometer, gyroscope, magnetometer, barometer, and a temperature sensor. A Sun Sensor also resides in this subsystem, separated from the breakout board.

The Communications Subsystem contains monitoring LEDs for the telemetry and payload as well as ports for an XBee Bluetooth Module or Long-Range Radio Chip, both are compatible for the same socket, and a GPS Receiver. An OLED screen may also be attached to view data collected.

Additionally, the CanSat houses a Payload Subsytem with labeled BUS terminals to give students the opportunity to integrate their own experiments into the existing power and microcontroller capabilities.

After working with Twiggs Space Labs to get several problems fixed, Nathan Eckhoff successfully connected the CanSat via Bluetooth to their Macbook and achieved a range of 50 feet from the laptop. We ordered a GP-735 GPS receiver to retrieve position telemetry from the constellation of GPS satellites, and Nathan Eckhoff installed the module via the port provided on the Communications Subsystem. After Ian Green returned prior to the semester, both students were able to identify the terminals required for the GPS receiver and acquired telemetry.

Currently we are attempting to integrate the GPS Arduino Sketch, Data Collection Arduino Sketch, and Datalogger Arduino Sketch into one so we may collect GPS and sensor data in future launch opportunities.
